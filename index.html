What is it?

Learn more
Kalachuchis are part of the Plumeria genus. They can be found in different variant in a lot of countries ranging from Meso-america, South America, to countries in the tropics like the Philippines, and even China. The trees where these flowers grow are deciduous, reaching up to 7m in height and contains a cream-like latex. The flowers are usually colored white with a yellow center or pink with the same center. They would almost always have 5 petals, but there are some with 6 or 4. It’s bark, leaves and flowers are useful in the medical field due to their properties, ex. Antifungal, Antioxidant, Cytotoxic.

The Kalachuchi Lane

The Kalachuchi Lane is a line of various Kalachuchi trees across the Grandstand beside the SHB. This area features many benches and tables where students can stay in their free time. There are times when umbrellas are added so that students are able to stay away from too much direct sunlight. There are many capiz lanterns hanging among the trees that light up after the sun sets. Usual disposing containers are scattered evenly through the area. This area gives them a wide view of the academic oval, and the field inside it. Although allowed by default, students are discouraged from climbing the trees, but we still climb it :)
